What We Believe

Better care starts with a clearer picture.

Most chiropractic offices start with treatment. We start with a conversation. Then a focused exam. Then, when it adds real clarity, digital X-rays and thermal imaging. Only after all of that do we recommend a care plan.

This is not about adding steps to the process. It is about making sure the care we recommend is actually built around your body, your structure, and your nervous system, not a generic template applied to everyone who walks in with back pain.

The spine is structural. The nervous system is functional. The body works as one connected system. Understanding that connection is what allows care to be specific, and specific care is what creates real, lasting improvement.

Core Values

The principles behind every patient visit.

01
Whole-System Function
We focus on how the spine, nervous system, and body work together, because isolated symptom treatment without understanding the connected system is often why problems keep returning.
02
Clear Answers
Patients should understand what is happening, why it matters, and what the care plan is designed to improve. Confusion is not part of good care.
03
Specific Correction
Care should be built around the patient's structure, nervous system stress patterns, symptoms, and goals. Tailored to you.
04
Better Function
The goal is not only to feel better. It is to help the body move better, adapt better, recover better, and function better in daily life, over time.
05
Technology with Purpose
Digital X-rays evaluate spinal structure. Thermal imaging observes nervous system stress patterns. Both are used only when they add real clarity, not as routine add-ons.
06
Local Trust
Life Charge is a local office serving Gallatin, Hendersonville, and surrounding Middle Tennessee communities. Dr. Palmer and Olivia are raising their family here. This is a long-term investment in this community.
In Practice

What the philosophy looks like on your first visit.

Philosophy is not useful if it does not show up in the room. Here is what our approach means for your actual experience at Life Charge Chiropractic.

1
Real time to talk
New patient visits run 45–60 minutes because listening is part of the exam. We do not rush through an intake form.
2
A focused exam, not a checklist
Dr. Palmer evaluates posture, spinal mechanics, neurological function, and movement specific to your complaint, not a generic protocol.
3
Imaging used to add clarity
When X-rays or thermal imaging would meaningfully improve understanding of your case, we use them. When they would not, we do not recommend them.
4
Recommendations explained, not assumed
You will know exactly what Dr. Palmer found, why it matters, and what the plan involves, before you commit to anything.
