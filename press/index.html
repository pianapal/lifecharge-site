About Dr. Palmer Piana

Bio (use any version that fits your space).

Short Bio (25 words)

Dr. Palmer Piana is a Doctor of Chiropractic, Board-Certified Thermologist, and children's book author serving families in Gallatin and Hendersonville, Tennessee.

Medium Bio (60 words)

Dr. Palmer Piana is the founder of Life Charge Chiropractic in Gallatin, TN. A Palmer College of Chiropractic graduate, he is a Board-Certified Thermologist, Blair Upper Cervical certified, and bilingual in English and Spanish. He is also the author of "The Day the Animals Found Their Wiggle: A Chiropractic Tale," a rhyming picture book about movement and the body for kids ages 3 to 8.

Long Bio (130 words)

Dr. Palmer Piana is the founder of Life Charge Chiropractic, a whole-system chiropractic practice in Gallatin, Tennessee, serving families across Sumner County and Middle Tennessee. He graduated from Palmer College of Chiropractic, the founding college of the profession, and is one of a small number of chiropractors in Middle Tennessee certified as a Board-Certified Thermologist and in Blair Upper Cervical Chiropractic. Dr. Palmer is bilingual in English and Spanish, plays rugby for a Nashville-area team, and serves on the Tennessee Chiropractic Association. In 2026 he published "The Day the Animals Found Their Wiggle: A Chiropractic Tale," a rhyming picture book about movement and the body for kids ages 3 to 8, illustrated by Farheen Fatima.

Public Health

The opioid alternative: what chiropractic care actually does

The Kazis 2019 BMJ Open study found chiropractic-first patients used dramatically fewer opioids. The Lancet 2018 series and the WHO 2023 guideline both recommend non-drug care first for low back pain. Dr. Palmer can speak to what evidence-based chiropractic looks like in practice.

Best for: health policy reporting, opioid crisis coverage, evidence-based medicine features
Family · Pediatric

"Mommy, why does Dr. Hoot adjust the giraffe?" Teaching kids about their bodies

The book includes characters representing real chiropractic concepts (stiffness, lost mobility, bodies that need help). Story angle on how books help children understand their own bodies and reduce anxiety about doctor visits.

Best for: parenting magazines, children's literature reviews, mommy blogs
