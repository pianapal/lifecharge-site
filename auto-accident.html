What to Watch For

Common symptoms after a collision.

Neck pain after a car accident
Often the first symptom, and one of the most common after a rear-end or side-impact collision. Can develop within hours or up to 72 hours later.
Headaches after a car accident
Post-accident headaches frequently originate in the upper cervical spine and base of the skull, not from head contact alone.
Upper back tension
The muscles, joints, and spinal segments of the upper back absorb significant force during a collision and often need evaluation.
Back pain after a car accident
Even low-speed collisions can cause lumbar stress. Low back pain after an accident deserves a careful structural exam.
Numbness or tingling after a crash
Radiating pain, tingling, or numbness into the arms or hands after a crash may indicate cervical nerve involvement that needs assessment.
Dizziness or brain fog after an accident
Dizziness, balance changes, and difficulty concentrating after a collision can be linked to upper cervical spine stress and warrant prompt evaluation.
No pain yet
If you were in an accident and feel fine right now, that does not mean nothing happened. Early evaluation gives you a clear baseline before symptoms develop.
Whiplash Care

Whiplash chiropractor in Gallatin & Hendersonville.

Whiplash happens when the head and neck are forced rapidly forward and back, stressing the soft tissue, ligaments, and joints of the cervical spine. It is the most common injury after a rear-end collision, and it does not require a high-speed crash to occur. Symptoms can include neck pain, stiffness, headaches, jaw discomfort, dizziness, and pain that radiates into the shoulders or arms, and they may not appear until 24 to 72 hours after the accident.

Early chiropractic evaluation matters with whiplash because untreated soft-tissue injuries can develop into chronic neck pain, restricted range of motion, and recurring headaches. We start with a focused exam and digital X-rays to rule out structural injury and identify the joints and segments under the most stress.

From there we build a specific care plan to restore motion, reduce inflammation, and address the underlying spinal stress, so the injury heals fully rather than becoming a long-term problem.

Why You Should Not Wait

The impact does not always hurt right away.

After a car accident, many people walk away feeling okay, only for neck pain, stiffness, headaches, or back pain to appear days or even weeks later. This is especially common with whiplash-type injuries, where the soft tissue and spinal structures are stressed but symptoms are delayed.

Even more important: a neck injury that gets shrugged off in the moment can quietly drive significant spinal degeneration years down the road. Untreated post-accident damage to the cervical spine often shows up later as chronic neck pain, recurring headaches, restricted range of motion, and accelerated arthritic changes on imaging. By the time it becomes a daily problem it is much harder to reverse.

Getting evaluated quickly matters. Early documentation of your injuries, through a focused chiropractic exam and digital X-rays, creates an objective record of what the accident did to your body. This protects you medically and legally, and it gives you the chance to address the injury while it is still recent.

Frequently Asked Questions

Auto accident chiropractic FAQ.

How soon should I see a chiropractor after a car accident?

As soon as possible, ideally within 72 hours. Whiplash and other soft-tissue injuries often have delayed onset, with neck pain, stiffness, and headaches showing up days after the collision. Early evaluation creates a documented baseline and lets us address injuries before they become chronic.

Does auto insurance cover chiropractic care?

In most cases, yes. Tennessee auto insurance policies often include MedPay or bodily injury coverage that pays for chiropractic care after an accident. We work directly with your auto insurance and the at-fault driver's insurance, and we can also accept personal injury liens when an attorney is involved.

What is MedPay and do you accept it?

MedPay (Medical Payments coverage) is an optional part of an auto insurance policy that pays for medical expenses after a crash, regardless of who was at fault. Yes, we accept MedPay, and in many cases it covers chiropractic care with no out-of-pocket cost to the patient.

Can a chiropractor treat whiplash?

Yes. Whiplash is one of the most common reasons people seek chiropractic care after a car accident. We perform a focused exam and digital X-rays, then build a specific care plan to restore range of motion, reduce inflammation, and address the underlying spinal stress.

I do not feel hurt after my accident. Should I still get checked?

Yes. Many post-accident injuries take 24 to 72 hours, sometimes longer, to produce symptoms, and minor neck injuries that get shrugged off in the moment can lead to significant spinal degeneration years later. An early exam establishes a baseline of your spinal alignment and protects you medically and legally if symptoms develop.

Do I need a referral or an attorney to come in?

No. You can come in directly, no referral required. If you are working with an attorney we are happy to coordinate documentation, and if you are not we can still help you understand your insurance options before care begins.
