How We Measure Progress

Progress tracked with more than symptoms.

Feeling better is a good start. But at Life Charge, we use objective markers to track how your body is actually responding to care over time.

1
Baseline thermal imaging
An initial scan establishes an objective picture of your nervous system stress patterns before care begins.
2
Functional re-evaluation
Periodic re-examination of posture, range of motion, and neurological function shows how your body is responding to the adjustments.
3
Progress thermal scans
Follow-up thermal imaging can show how surface temperature patterns compare with the baseline scan over time.
4
Report of progress and updated recommendations
Dr. Palmer walks you through what has changed, what still needs attention, and what the next phase of care looks like, in plain language.
