Why I Wrote It

A book that explains chiropractic the way kids understand the world.

As a chiropractor and a dad, I wanted my kids, and the kids of my patients, to have a way to understand what happens in our office without a single confusing medical word. Children do not need lectures about the nervous system. They need a story.

In the book, the animals of Sunny Meadow lose their wiggle. Kiki the kangaroo cannot bounce. Gigi the giraffe cannot stretch. Leo the lion cub cannot roar. Slinky the snake cannot slither smoothly. They each get stuck in the way real bodies get stuck. And Dr. Hoot, a wise owl, helps them get back to feeling like themselves with gentle, specific care.

The whole book is built on the same idea that runs my practice. Your body has the power to feel and move well. Sometimes it just needs a little help getting clear of what is in the way. The kids who hear this book read at bedtime grow up understanding their bodies in a way most adults never do.

What Kids Take Away

Big ideas, little reader.

Each rhyming page introduces a real chiropractic concept in a story-first, kid-first way. By the end, kids understand more about how their bodies work than most adults remember from anatomy class.

01

Movement is power

Bouncing, stretching, roaring, and slithering are how each animal expresses who they are. When movement is stuck, something in the body is asking for help.

02

The spine matters

The book introduces the idea that a spine that gets stuck in the wrong way can change how the whole body feels and moves, in language a four-year-old can hold.

03

It is okay to ask for help

Each animal is a little embarrassed at first. Dr. Hoot makes it normal to say "I do not feel right" and to let someone trustworthy take a careful look.

04

Care is gentle, not scary

Dr. Hoot uses a tap, a gentle press, a careful poke. The book sets the expectation that being adjusted is calm, specific, and quick. No fear.

05

Each animal is different

Kiki, Gigi, Leo, and Slinky each have different problems and need different help. The story models that real care is specific to the patient, not one-size-fits-all.

06

Your body has its own light

The recurring image of a glowing wiggle along each animal's spine is a kid-friendly stand-in for the nervous system. Care turns the light back on.

Meet The Animals

The cast of Sunny Meadow.

Page 2

Kiki the Kangaroo

"Her hops had lost their hip-hoorah."

Kiki used to bounce sky-high, until one big morning jump landed wrong. Her spring is gone, and she wants it back. Stands in for that feeling we all know when a movement we used to do easily suddenly feels off.

Page 3

Gigi the Giraffe

"It felt so stiff, and stuck, and tight."

Gigi twisted while she slept and woke with a stiff neck. She cannot reach the high leaves anymore. A great character to introduce kids to the idea that posture and sleep position matter.

Page 4

Leo the Lion Cub

"His voice was squeaky, weak, and sad."

Leo lost his roar after wrestling with his dad. Hides behind his paw, embarrassed. Speaks to kids who feel quiet or off after a tumble at school or a rough day on the playground.

Page 5

Slinky the Snake

"He bent too sharp and got a crick."

Slinky chased a frog through a tight log and got a kink that stuck. Wears a detective hat. Helps kids understand that bodies can get out of alignment from one specific moment.

The Wise One

Dr. Hoot

"He didn't bring a bag of pills, just knowledge, heart, and healing skills."

An elderly owl with spectacles, a satchel of scrolls, and feathers that look like a doctor's coat. The chiropractor stand-in. Shows kids what a thoughtful, gentle, healing-focused doctor looks like.

For Parents and Caregivers

Every page mirrors a real chiropractic concept.

If you have ever wondered how to explain chiropractic to your kids, this book does the work for you. Each animal's stuck spot maps to a real pattern we see in the office every week. Stiff neck. Lost spring. A specific moment that left things tight.

Dr. Hoot's "adjustments" in the book are described as a tap, a gentle press, a careful poke. That language is intentional. It sets up children to expect care that is gentle, specific, and brief. The kids of our patients who have grown up with this book come into their own first chiropractic visits calm, curious, and ready.

The closing message of the book is the same one I tell my adult patients: your power lives in you, from tip of tail to top of head. Keep it clear and let it shine. Bodies are built to move and heal. Care helps clear the interference standing in the way.

If your child is curious about what happens at our office, or if you want to introduce the idea of pediatric chiropractic care in a friendly, non-clinical way, this book is for them.

Common Questions

About the book.

What ages is the book for?
Best fit is roughly ages 3 to 8. Younger kids enjoy the rhymes, the bright animal characters, and the read-along feel. Older kids pick up on the meaning, ask great questions, and start connecting it to their own bodies.
Where can I buy it?
It is available on Amazon as a paperback. Click here to grab a copy. Some Life Charge Chiropractic patients also pick up signed copies at the office.
Do I need to be a Life Charge patient to enjoy the book?
Not at all. The book stands on its own as a fun, rhyming read about movement, animals, and listening to your body. Plenty of families enjoy it without ever stepping foot in a chiropractic office.
Is it good for kids who are nervous about doctor visits?
Yes. Dr. Hoot is gentle, kind, and listens carefully. Many parents tell us the book helped their child go into a first chiropractic, dental, or pediatrician visit calmer and more curious than they otherwise would have.
Are there more books coming?
More stories from Sunny Meadow are in the works. If you would like to be notified, email the office or follow Life Charge Chiropractic on social.
Can I get a signed copy?
Yes. Stop by Life Charge Chiropractic at 1921 Nashville Pike, Gallatin, TN, or pick one up after your appointment. Dr. Palmer is happy to personalize it for the kids in your life.
