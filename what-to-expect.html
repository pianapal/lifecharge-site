Step by Step

Your first visit is simple.

1
Tell us what is going on
Your first visit starts with a real conversation. We listen to your symptoms, your history, what makes things better or worse, and what you have already tried. Nothing is rushed. This context shapes everything that follows.
2
Focused chiropractic examination
Dr. Palmer performs a thorough chiropractic evaluation, posture assessment, range of motion testing, neurological and orthopedic screening, and a hands-on spinal exam. This is not a generic checklist. It is a focused look at how your body is functioning.
3
Digital X-rays and thermal imaging
When clinically appropriate, we use digital X-rays to evaluate spinal alignment and structure, and thermal imaging to observe nervous system stress patterns. These tools give us objective information that changes how care is recommended and delivered.
4
Report of findings and clear recommendations
Dr. Palmer walks you through everything found, what the exam showed, what the imaging revealed, and what it means for your body. You will receive clear recommendations before any care begins, so you can make a confident, informed decision.
Before You Come In

What to bring and what to expect.

New patient visits at Life Charge run about 45–60 minutes. We want enough time to actually listen, examine, and explain, not rush through a checklist.

Any prior X-rays or MRI reports if you have them
A list of current medications or supplements
Insurance card and ID if using insurance
Accident or injury documentation if applicable
Comfortable clothing you can move easily in
A clear description of your main concern and how long it has been present

Common Questions

Honest answers before you book.

How long is the first visit?
New patient visits run about 45–60 minutes. We budget enough time to listen, examine thoroughly, and explain what we find, not rush through a checklist.
Will I get adjusted on my first visit?
Not always. The first visit is primarily an evaluation. In some cases Dr. Palmer may begin gentle care on the same visit, but only after the exam is complete and findings are reviewed. Nothing happens without your understanding and consent.
Do I need a referral?
No. You can schedule directly with Life Charge Chiropractic without a physician referral. If you have insurance or are coming in after an accident, bring those details and the front desk will help sort through the paperwork.
Do you take insurance?
We offer transparent payment options and will help you understand what may be covered. All costs are discussed and explained before care begins, no surprises.
What if I'm nervous about chiropractic?
That is completely understandable, and a good reason to start with a conversation. Dr. Palmer explains everything before doing anything. There is no pressure to commit to care before you feel ready and informed.
