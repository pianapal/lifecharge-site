When You Were Hurt At Work

Work injuries deserve specific care from day one.

A workplace injury is not a normal sore back. It usually involves a specific incident, a specific mechanism, and a specific set of structures that took the load. The right care starts with understanding exactly what happened and what your body shows on exam, then building a plan that addresses both the injury and the documentation that comes with it.

At Life Charge Chiropractic, we evaluate how your spine, joints, soft tissues, and nervous system have responded to the injury. We document objective findings carefully, communicate clearly with employers, adjusters, and attorneys when needed, and work with you on a return-to-work plan that protects what is healing.

The goal is not just to get you through the claim. The goal is to find the root of the injury and address it specifically, so it does not become a chronic problem.

What Patients Describe

Common work injury presentations.

Lifting injury
Lifting a heavy object, often awkwardly or fast, that produced a sudden pop, pull, or sharp pain in the back, neck, or shoulder.
Slip, trip, or fall
A fall on the job that produced bruising, jarring, or twisting injury to the spine, hip, knee, or shoulder, with symptoms that have not resolved.
Repetitive strain
Pain that built up over weeks or months from repeated motion, lifting, typing, or awkward positioning at work, with no single incident.
Pushing or pulling injury
Loaded carts, equipment, or stuck objects that required force and produced low back, mid back, or shoulder pain that has not settled.
Vehicle-related work injury
Pain that started after a work-related collision, jarring on rough roads, or extended hours behind the wheel as part of the job.
Symptoms that worsened with light duty
Pain that did not respond to rest, modified work, or general care, and is starting to affect sleep, focus, and quality of life at home.
Our Approach

Specific care, careful documentation, clear communication.

Work injury care has two parts. The clinical work, which addresses what the injury actually did to your body. The documentation work, which protects your claim and your access to ongoing care. We take both seriously.

1
Incident and injury history
We document exactly when, where, and how the injury happened, what you felt at the time, and what has changed since. The detail matters for both care and the claim.
2
Focused exam and objective findings
Orthopedic testing, range of motion, neurological screening, and a hands-on chiropractic exam, with findings recorded in a way that supports the claim file.
3
Digital imaging when clinically appropriate
When the exam, your symptoms, or the mechanism of injury point to something imaging would clarify, we use digital X-rays and refer for advanced imaging when warranted.
4
Specific care plan and clear reporting
You will know exactly what was found, what it means, what the plan is, and what the duty restrictions look like. We share that information with the parties who need it.

Beyond the Standard Exam

What we look for in a work injury exam.

Work-comp injuries need more than a quick checkup. These are the specific things we evaluate so the care plan and the documentation both match what is actually going on.

01Incident

Detailed incident documentation

A clear, specific record of what happened, when, where, and what you felt at the time. This is foundational for both the clinical picture and the claim.

02Mechanism

Injury mechanism analysis

The way the body was loaded tells us which structures are most likely involved. A bent and twisted lift loads the discs differently than a fall onto a hip or a sudden push.

03Tissue

Soft tissue versus structural injury

Strains, sprains, joint sprains, disc involvement, and nerve irritation each behave differently. We sort out which is which so the care plan and timeline are realistic.

04Workers Comp

Work-comp protocol experience

Work-comp care has its own rhythm of authorizations, notes, and reporting. We know how the system works and we keep your file clean and current.

05Communication

Employer and insurer communication

Adjusters, employers, and attorneys often need updates. We send timely, clear, professional reports so the people who need information can act on it.

06Return To Work

Return-to-work readiness

Returning too early can re-injure. Returning too late can stall recovery. We assess function and tolerance objectively so the timing matches what your body can actually handle.

07Restrictions

Duty levels and restrictions

Lifting limits, sitting and standing tolerances, and modified-duty recommendations spelled out clearly so your employer has what they need to support you safely.

08Prevention

Prevention of re-injury

As you return to full duty, we work on the structural and movement patterns that left you vulnerable in the first place, so the next lift, push, or fall does not put you back here.

Why Early Care Matters

Documented, specific care from day one prevents chronic disability.

Work-comp care is different from general chiropractic care, and patients deserve a provider who understands both the clinical side and the system side. The clinical side is what your body actually needs to recover. The system side is what the claim, the employer, and the insurer need to keep care moving and to protect your rights.

Objective findings matter more in work-comp than almost anywhere else in healthcare. A specific exam with measurable range of motion, neurological screening, orthopedic testing, and clear notes is the foundation of a credible claim and a credible care plan. We document carefully because it serves you both clinically and administratively.

There is also a window of opportunity with work injuries. The cases that become chronic disability cases are usually the ones that did not get specific care early. The body adopts compensations. Pain patterns lock in. Other structures take on load they were not built for. The longer that goes on, the harder it is to undo. Early, specific, well-documented care is one of the best things you can do for the long-term outcome.

When the exam tells us what is actually driving your symptoms, the care plan can be specific. Specific care holds longer, because it changes the underlying mechanics rather than just managing the pain.

Common Questions

Work injury chiropractic FAQ.

Do you accept workers' comp?
Yes. Life Charge Chiropractic accepts workers' compensation cases for back, neck, shoulder, and other work-related injuries. We are familiar with the process, the documentation requirements, and how to coordinate with adjusters, employers, and attorneys.
Do I need approval before coming in?
It depends on the claim and the carrier. Some adjusters require pre-authorization before chiropractic care begins. Others do not. We can help you figure out where your case stands and what steps to take, including reaching out to the adjuster on your behalf.
What documentation do you provide?
A detailed initial exam report, ongoing visit notes with objective findings, periodic progress reports, duty restriction recommendations, and any specific reports the adjuster, employer, or attorney requests. Documentation is one of the things we take most seriously in work-comp cases.
Can I see a chiropractor for a work injury?
Yes. Tennessee allows employees with work-related injuries to receive chiropractic care, often as part of an approved provider list or after referral. We help patients understand their options under their specific claim and coordinate with the carrier as needed.
What if my injury was years ago?
Older work injuries can still be evaluated and treated. The exam will tell us what the original injury left behind, what compensations have built up, and what is realistic now. Whether the case is still open under the original claim depends on your specific situation, and we can help you sort that out.
Will you communicate with my employer or attorney?
Yes, with your written permission. We send timely, clear, professional updates to the parties who need them, including adjusters, employers, attorneys, and other treating providers. Good communication keeps care moving and protects your interests.
