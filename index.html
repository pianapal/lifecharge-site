How We Work

Whole-system care, step by step.

Better care starts with a clearer picture. On your first visit we work through three assessments, none of it generic, so the plan is built around what is actually happening in your body.

Postural & spinal exam

A hands-on, palpatory exam to feel for joint restriction, tension patterns, and where the structure is compensating. Combined with a posture check that shows how everything is working together when you stand and move.

Thermal imaging

A whole-back thermal scan shows where your spine is putting stress on the nervous system. It reveals what is actually driving the symptoms, not just where you feel them.

Digital X-rays

When the picture is incomplete, our digital X-ray system finds curves, degeneration, or disc-space issues you cannot see any other way. We only use X-rays when clinically appropriate, so the plan is built on what is actually there.

From there, you sit down with Dr. Palmer and walk through what we found, what it means, and what we recommend, before any care begins.

Common Questions

Honest answers before you book.

What happens on my first visit?
You get a real conversation about what you are dealing with, a focused chiropractic exam, and, when clinically appropriate, digital X-rays and thermal imaging. Then Dr. Palmer explains what he sees and what he recommends before any care begins.
Do I need a referral?
No. You can book directly with Life Charge Chiropractic. If you have insurance or are coming in after an accident, bring those details and the front desk will help.
Is chiropractic safe for kids and pregnancy?
Yes, when done thoughtfully. Dr. Palmer adjusts the technique to the patient, gentler approaches for children and modified positioning for pregnancy. You will always know what is being done and why.
How long is a typical appointment?
New patient visits run about 45–60 minutes so there is real time to listen, examine, and explain. Routine follow-ups are shorter and focused on the care plan.
Do you take insurance?
We offer transparent payment options and can help you understand what is covered. Costs and recommendations are explained before care begins so there are no surprises.
