Why You Should Not Wait

The impact does not always hurt right away.

After a car accident, many people walk away feeling okay, only for neck pain, stiffness, headaches, or back pain to appear days or even weeks later. This is especially common with whiplash-type injuries, where the soft tissue and spinal structures are stressed but symptoms are delayed.

Getting evaluated quickly matters. Early documentation of your injuries, through a focused chiropractic exam, digital X-rays, and thermal imaging when appropriate, creates an objective record of what the accident did to your body. This protects you medically and legally.

At Life Charge Chiropractic, we work with patients after auto accidents to provide a clear, specific, and well-documented care plan from day one.

What to Watch For

Common symptoms after a collision.

Neck pain and stiffness
Often the first symptom, and one of the most common after a rear-end or side-impact collision. Can develop within hours or up to 72 hours later.
Headaches
Post-accident headaches frequently originate in the upper cervical spine and base of the skull, not from head contact alone.
Upper back tension
The muscles, joints, and spinal segments of the upper back absorb significant force during a collision and often need evaluation.
Low back pain
Even low-speed collisions can cause lumbar stress. Low back pain after an accident deserves a careful structural exam.
Arm or shoulder symptoms
Radiating pain, tingling, or numbness into the arms after a crash may indicate cervical nerve involvement that needs assessment.
No pain yet
If you were in an accident and feel fine right now, that does not mean nothing happened. Early evaluation gives you a clear baseline before symptoms develop.
Our Approach After an Accident

A specific, documented evaluation from day one.

We do not use a generic post-accident protocol. We evaluate your body specifically, based on the mechanics of the collision, your symptoms, and what the exam reveals.

1
Accident history and symptom timeline
We document the collision details, when symptoms started, and how they have progressed, all of which matters for your records.
2
Full chiropractic examination
Spinal, neurological, and orthopedic testing to identify which structures were stressed and how severely.
3
Digital X-rays and thermal imaging
Objective imaging that documents spinal alignment and nervous system stress patterns, and can be used to track your progress over time.
4
Specific care plan and clear documentation
Your care is built around your findings, not a one-size-fits-all post-accident template. All findings are clearly documented from day one.
