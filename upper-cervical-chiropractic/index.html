The Upper Cervical Spine

The top of the spine affects the whole system.

The atlas (C1) and axis (C2) are unlike any other vertebrae in the spine. They are highly mobile, they house the brainstem, and they sit at the junction where the skull meets the spine. Even subtle misalignment at this level can affect head posture, nervous system function, and how the rest of the spine compensates below.

Upper cervical chiropractic focuses specifically on this region, using precise analysis, careful evaluation, and gentle, targeted adjustments to correct misalignment at the atlas and axis level. The goal is to restore proper positioning, reduce nervous system interference, and allow the body to function more effectively from the top down.

At Life Charge Chiropractic, upper cervical care begins with a thorough evaluation including cervical X-rays when appropriate. Dr. Palmer uses the objective findings from X-rays and thermal imaging to guide exactly where, how, and when to adjust, not guesswork.

What Upper Cervical Care May Help

Conditions with a potential upper cervical component.

Recurring headaches
Many cervicogenic headaches, including those at the base of the skull, trace back to atlas and axis misalignment compressing the suboccipital nerves.
Neck pain and stiffness
Chronic cervical restriction, particularly one-sided, often involves upper cervical joint dysfunction that does not resolve with general neck stretching alone.
Postural imbalance
A tilted atlas changes how the head sits on the spine, which can create compensatory patterns throughout the cervical, thoracic, and lumbar spine.
Dizziness and vertigo
The upper cervical spine has direct mechanoreceptor input into the brain's balance centers. Misalignment here can contribute to cervicogenic dizziness and balance disturbance.
Jaw and TMJ discomfort
The atlas and the temporomandibular joint are closely connected anatomically and neurologically. Upper cervical misalignment can influence jaw mechanics.
Whole-body nervous system stress
Because the brainstem passes through the upper cervical region, atlas misalignment can have downstream effects on the nervous system beyond just local neck symptoms.
Our Approach

Specific analysis. Gentle, precise correction.

Upper cervical care is not the same as a general cervical adjustment. It requires specific X-ray analysis and a precise, low-force approach targeted to the atlas and axis, not the whole neck.

1
Detailed history and cervical evaluation
We assess head posture, atlas positioning, neurological function, and any symptoms that may indicate upper cervical involvement.
2
Cervical X-rays for precise analysis
Lateral and open-mouth (odontoid) X-ray views give us the exact information needed to understand atlas and axis alignment, and guide the correction.
3
Thermal imaging for nervous system patterns
Upper cervical misalignment often shows in thermal asymmetry. Thermal imaging helps confirm nervous system involvement and track how the body responds to correction.
4
Gentle, specific upper cervical adjustment
Low-force, precisely directed correction at the atlas and axis, with follow-up evaluation to confirm the correction has held and the nervous system is responding.
