Beyond the Standard Exam

What we look for after a Hendersonville-area crash.

The ER rules out the immediate emergencies and sends you home. Real post-accident injuries hide in the gaps. These are the things we specifically test for that often go undetected after a Hendersonville-area collision.

01 Imaging

Ligament instability

Motion-study X-rays (flexion, extension, and lateral bending views) reveal damaged or stretched neck ligaments that standard static films miss. Untreated ligament injury is one of the leading causes of chronic post-accident pain and a major reason settlements drag on.

02 Neuro

Concussion screen (SWAY in-office)

We run the SWAY concussion protocol, a validated balance and neurocognitive screen, right in the office. Many post-accident concussions are missed because nobody tested cognition, balance, or reaction time. SWAY documents it objectively.

03 Imaging

Disc & spinal cord injuries

When the exam suggests a disc herniation, nerve root involvement, or possible cord injury, we coordinate an MRI. These injuries do not show on X-ray and they completely change the right course of care.

04 Spine

Upper cervical (C1/C2) involvement

A common driver of post-accident headaches, dizziness, and brain fog. The upper cervical spine is rarely evaluated in detail at the ER or in standard urgent care, and missing it is one of the most common reasons patients stay symptomatic for months.

05 Spine

Loss of cervical curve

Even minor collisions on Vietnam Veterans Pkwy or Hwy 31E can flatten or reverse the natural curve of the neck. That structural change accelerates degeneration over time. We measure curve angles on lateral imaging and track them across the course of care.

06 Whiplash

Whiplash-associated disorder grading

A formal Quebec Task Force classification of whiplash severity (WAD I through IV) that informs the care plan, activity restrictions, and prognosis. Often skipped by providers without specific motor vehicle injury training.
