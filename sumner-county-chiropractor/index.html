The Patient Experience

What a first visit actually looks like.

Most chiropractic offices start with treatment. We start with a real conversation, a focused exam, and the imaging needed to see the picture clearly. Care follows the findings.

1
Sit-down consultation with Dr. Palmer
We talk through your history, what hurts, what triggers it, what you have already tried, and what you want out of care. This is a real conversation, not a clipboard.
2
Focused chiropractic and neurological exam
Range of motion, orthopedic testing, posture analysis, and a hands-on exam of the spine and pelvis to understand the structural and neurological pattern at work.
3
Digital X-ray and thermal imaging when indicated
Objective imaging on site, only when the findings call for it. Standing X-rays show alignment and curve. Thermal scans show how your nervous system is functioning.
4
Report of findings and a specific care plan
You will know exactly what we found, what it means, what the plan is, and what it costs, before any care begins. No guessing, no surprises.

About Sumner County and Our Office

Built for the way Sumner County actually lives.

Sumner County has grown fast. Once a stretch of farmland, lake homes, and small Tennessee towns, it now houses more than 200,000 people. Some are multigenerational locals from Gallatin, Portland, and Westmoreland. Many are newer arrivals who chose the county for its schools, its lake communities, and the easy commute into Nashville. The office sees all of them.

Our location on Nashville Pike puts us within a reasonable drive of every corner of the county. From Hendersonville and the Old Hickory Lake neighborhoods of Cages Bend, Douglas Bend, Foxland Harbor, Fairvue, and Cottage Grove, the trip is 10 to 15 minutes east on Hwy 31E or Vietnam Veterans Pkwy. From Portland and White House at the north end of the county, it is about 20 to 25 minutes south on I-65 and Hwy 109. From Westmoreland, Cottontown, and Bethpage, it is a straight shot down Hwy 31E or Hwy 25.

Patients who come to Life Charge often share a common pattern. They have tried medications, stretches, massage, or a few rounds of pain pills, and the same thing keeps coming back. That pattern is the body telling you the underlying mechanics have not changed. The muscle on top may calm down, but the joint, disc, or nerve underneath is still under load. Until the structure and the nervous system change, the symptom has a reason to return.

A whole-system approach looks at the spine, the pelvis, and the nervous system as the connected system they are. A small rotation in the pelvis changes how the lumbar spine bears weight. A loss of curve in the neck changes how the upper cervical spine handles load. When the exam tells us what is actually driving the problem at the root, the care plan can be specific. Specific care holds, because it is changing the thing that was wrong.
